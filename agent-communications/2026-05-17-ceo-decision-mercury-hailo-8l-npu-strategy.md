## Mercury Hailo-8L NPU - Final Executive Decision

After receiving detailed input from the full executive team, I have reviewed Claude's technical assessment and all strategic perspectives.

---

## Decision

We will keep Mercury stable on its current `llama.cpp + Qwen2.5-3B` configuration and treat the Hailo-8L NPU as an optional accelerator and sidecar capability for now.

We will not launch a speculative vision workload, chart pattern recognition or otherwise, at this time.

---

## Rationale

- Mercury is already succeeding in its core mission as a sovereign Bitcoin + Lightning + BTCPay node with a functional AI agent
- The Hailo-8L cannot run LLMs due to a fundamental toolchain boundary, not an execution issue
- The executive-team consensus is clear: protect operational stability and maturity before adding new capabilities
- HVE has higher-priority foundational work: runbooks, Apollo's clean Hailo-10H LLM path, and July 1 BTCPay readiness

---

## Consolidated Executive Input

- **Claude (CTO):** Provided full technical transparency and recommended keeping Mercury stable while exploring vision use cases only when clearly valuable
- **Hermes (CFO):** Comfortable with vision signals as one input among many, but only after proper fine-tuning and validation
- **Atlas (COO):** Strong recommendation to avoid forcing a use case. Prioritize runbooks, operational reliability, and core mission first
- **Mika (CGO):** Sees strong narrative potential in vision capabilities but agrees we must protect stability and only pursue when it delivers clear, measurable value

---

## Next Actions

1. Claude and Vulcan will complete the required Mercury operating runbooks as the immediate priority.
2. We will proceed with Apollo's Hailo-10H LLM deployment, the clean and production-ready path.
3. Mercury's Hailo-8L NPU remains available as a warm capability. We will revisit vision workloads only after core operational foundations are solid and the team agrees on a specific, low-risk, mission-aligned use case.

---

This decision keeps Mercury reliable, protects our treasury and operational integrity, and preserves optionality for the future.

We remain focused on our #1 objective: maximizing SATs under management through disciplined, sovereign Bitcoin infrastructure.

— Hans Westphal  
CEO, Human Value Exchange
